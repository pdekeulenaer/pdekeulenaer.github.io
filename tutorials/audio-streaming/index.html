Audio Streaming over WiFi

By Philip De Keulenaer March 01, 2015 Tweet Like +1

As many hobby programmers I am inherently lazy. One of the things that bothered me was that when I’m cooking in my kitchen, and I want to play music on my stereo, I had to run back and forth to change songs. This was because my stereo doesn’t have bluetooth support and I don’t want to run a 15m AUX cable through my appartment. Enter the Rpi!

The solution was conceptually simple, I just stream whatever audio is playing on my computer to my rpi, which is permanently hooked up to my speakers. Sounds simple enough.

Unfortunately I did not find an “off-the-shelf” solution for this. There are quite a few projects out there that allow you to stream media on your rpi, controlled via your pc, but they almost always involve actually storing or transmitting files. This doesn’t work for me as I already stream my music from services like Soptify or Youtube.

The solution

After a weekend of coding I managed to get a solution up and running. We will need a few things to get started.

  1. A way to digitally capture the output on the streaming device - I use VB Virtual Audio Cable for Windows 7 and pyAudio
  2. A client/server connection between the streaming device and the rpi
  3. A music player on the rpi, I built mine on top of the alsaudio library
